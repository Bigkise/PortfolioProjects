The Scenario

The Director of marketing of Cyclistic, Lily Moreno, believes that the next step to company success is to maximize the number of annual memberships. So the goal of the marketing analyst team is to understand how causal riders and annual riders differ in their use of the Cyclistic bikes. From these insights, the analytics team could be able to design a new marketing strategy to incentivize casual riders to become annual members.

Three questions will be answered.

  1. How do annual members and casual riders use Cyclistic bikes differently?
  2. Why would casual riders buy Cyclistic annual memberships?
  3. How can Cyclistic use digital media to influence casual riders to become member?

I have been assigned by Moreno the first question.

Conclusions/Summary of insights

Members and casual riders differ in how long they use, how often they use the bikes, and on which days of the week does every group peak while using Cyclistic Bikes:

  • Casual rides peak during weekends (plot3). There is a higher probability they are tourists visiting and sightseeing in the city, or they are your normal Chicago citizens who are riding bikes on their leisure time during the weekend. The longer average ride time for casual riders (plot2), also peaking at the weekend, provides evidence.
  • Ride lengths for members are relatively shorter compared to casual riders. This could be clarified as such, most members use the bikes to commute on workdays. This clarification would also explain the short riding duration of members. They ride for necessity and usually the same ride lengths and the same distance.
  • More than 50% of the riders are annual members (plots5), suggesting that the company has already achieved a good level of loyalty among its bike users. This indicates a positive message, namely that the company is going to convince many casual riders to become premium members, and keep their new members satisfied.

Recommendations

Give discounts for longer rides when you have a membership Longer rides should fall under a type of rewards program when they become members

  • The Marketing campaign should be launched between February to August, as the number of trips made by casual riders peak around those months in the year.
  • As casual riders usage reaches its highest point on the weekend, the marketing campaign should include weekend-only deals at a discounted price. This could incentivize casual riders to become premium members
  • The campaign could include a ride more, pay less incentive plan or riding more unlocks specific discount vouchers.
